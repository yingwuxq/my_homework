The old man sat beneath the cherry blossoms, watching pink petals dance in the autumn wind. Each falling flower reminded him of time's gentle cruelty - beautiful, fleeting, and utterly unstoppable.

"Why do we love knowing we will lose?" he whispered.

The wind answered with silence, carrying another petal into the golden sunset.

Perhaps love's meaning isn't in forever, but in the courage to hold something fragile, knowing your hands will one day open. Like stars that die to become stardust, like rivers that empty into seas - we are all temporary, and that is what makes us precious.

In the end, we are just moments strung together by memory and hope. And maybe, just maybe, that is enough.
